Final Design of Prototype

The final prototype design has made it improvement from the previous two prototype models.

Description

Final design represents the final and most refined version of the ESP32-C3 Zero enclosure. The case is 3D-printed with a compact, box-like geometry and a two-part structure (top lid and base.)

The top surface is flat and features three curved ventilation slots arranged in a circular pattern, similar to Prototype 2 but cleaner and more integrated. The enclosure is fully closed, providing strong protection for the internal electronic components.

On the side, arectangular cut-out is precisely positioned, allowing access to external connections such as a USB port or GPIO interface. The bottom portion appears to include a defined internal support structure (ledge or seating area) at hold the ESP32-C3 Zero board securely in place.

Compared to earlier versions, the edges, proportions, and openings are more consistent, indicating improved dimensional accuracy and printing control. Although minor surface texture from FDM printing is still visible, the overall finish is more uniform.

Key features

  • Fully optimized enclosure: Completely enclosed design ensures maximum protection. Prevents dust exposure and accidental damage.
  • Refined ventilation system: Curved slots provide ensures maximum protection. Prevents dust exposure and accidental damage.
  • Accurate port accessibility: Side cutout is better aligned and more precise. Supports easy connection without removing the case.
  • Secure internal support: Internal lip/structure ensures stable PCB placement. Reduces movement compared to earlier prototypes.
  • Improved structural strength: Thick, consistent walls enhance durability. Balanced design prevents weak points.
  • More professional finish: Cleaner edges and smoother layers. Looks closer to a production-ready product.

Conclusion

Final Design demonstrates a successful and complete evolution of the ESP32-C3 Zero case design.

  • Prototype 1 focused on concept validation but lacked protection and structure plus the size.
  • Prototype 2 introduced enclosure, ventilation, and usability improvements.
  • Final Design refines all elements into a well-balanced, practical product
