Prototype 1

The first design ever to be 3D printed made by the students who are tasked work on ESP32-C3 Zero

Description

Prototype 1 appears to be an early 3D-printed enclosure for the ESP32-C3 Zero board, likely printed using FDM 3d printing.

  • The outer structure is a rectangle frame with thicks, intended to house and protect the board.
  • The case includes a large rectangular cut-out window on one side, likely for: USB port access, GPIO pins or ventilation.
  • The base inside the frame shows layered infill lines, indicating the printing pattern and structural support.

This prototype primarily demonstrates basic fit, structure, and concept validation, rather than final aesthetics.

Pros

  • Functional design: Provides basic enclosure protection for the ESP32-C3 board.
  • Rapid prototyping success: Successfully demonstrates form factor and dimension conecept.
  • Lightweight and simple: Minimal matieral usage reduces print time and cost.

Error

  • Print quality issues: Stringing along edges, uneven surface finish inside the base and possible over-extrusion or poor layer adhesion.
  • Dimensional roughness: Edges and openings appear slightly incosistent or rough, which may affect: proper fitting of the PCB and connector alignment.
  • Lack of internal features: No dedicated support for, PCB positioning, standoff height alignment, board may move inside the case.

Conclusion

Prototype 1 serves as a successful proof-of-concept enclosure for the ESP32-C3 Zero. It demonstrates fundamental design idea and confirms that the overall size and layout are workable.

However, it is clearly an early-stage prototype, with room for improvement in both:

  • Mechanical design (mounting features, structure)
  • Print quality (tuning settings, clearner finish)
