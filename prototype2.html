Prototype 2

The second prototype design made to be bit of improvement and little bit of bigger.

Description

Prototype 2 shows a more refined 3D-printed enclosure for the ESP32-C3 Zero, the design is noticeably improved in both structure and finishing.

  • The case has a fully enclosed top surface, unlike Prototype 1's large open cut-out.
  • A circular ventilation pattern (fan-like curved slots arranged symmetrically) is integrated into the top panel.
  • The top face appears flat and uniform, indicating improved print quality and layer consistency.
  • The side structure appears more complete, suggesting a two-part or thicker enclosure design.
  • Surface texture still shows slight from FDM printing but is more consistent than 2.

This prototype moves beyond concept validation and begins focusing on functionality, protection, and usability.

Pros

  • Improved structural integrity: Fully enclosed top increases strength and durability Better protection against dust and accidental contact.
  • Ventilation design: The circular vent pattern. allows airflow while maintaining enclosure safety. Helps with heat dissipation, important for ESP32-C3 Zero board.
  • Better print quality: Smoother and more consistent top surface. Reduced stringing compared to Prototype 1.
  • More professional appearance: Cleaner geometry and symmetric vent pattern give a more polished and finished look.
  • Functional balance: Successfully balances of protection, accessibility (through controlled opening), cooling.

Error

  • Surface finish still rough: Slight grainy or speckled texture vislbe on the top surface.
  • Venting opening precision: The curved slots appear slightly uneven or not perfectly sharp. Could be caused by low reolution, print speed and cooling settings.
  • Port alignment not visible It is unclear whether USB/GPIO openings are well aligned, which is critical for usability.

Conclusion

Prototype 1 represents a clear and significant improvement over Prototype 1. The design has evolved simple open frame into a more complete and protective enclosure, with added attention to airflow and durability.

  • Introduction of ventilation features
